Green Party

Economy

The Green Party would grow the economy by shifting it to a “green economy”; create jobs in renewable energy and efficiency.

  • Eliminate personal taxes on incomes below low-income cut-off of $20,000
  • Reduce federal small business tax rate to 9% by 2019
  • Raise corporate taxes over four years from the current level of 15% to 19%
  • Work with provinces to increase taxes on tobacco and alcohol.
  • Create jobs by making homes and business more energy efficient
  • Increase federal minimum wage to $15/hour and introduce legislation to ban unpaid internships

Bill C-51

Will completely repeal Bill C-51 saying it “Is the death of Freedom.”

  • Repeal Bill C-51, the anti-terror act and the portions of Bills C-13 and S-4 that allow warrantless disclosure
  • Introduce a comprehensive security plan that will provide coordinated direction to the RCMP, CSIS, Canada Border Services Agency, Coast Guard and Foreign Affairs, Trade and Development Canada
  • End the Canadian government’s online surveillance program and introduce legislation to "defend an internet that is open and free, where Canadians have a right to privacy."

Syria and Iraq Crisis

The Green Party believes that the Conservatives are not doing nearly as much as they can to help. "We need to get back to the kind of country we were where we welcomed people and we didn't allow a humanitarian crisis to be wrapped up in red tape," May said. She would like to see Canada do “everything it can” to help the crisis. However, she has not specified how she will help the refugees.

  • End Canada’s combat role in Iraq and Syria and seek other means to defeat ISIS
  • A Green government would strengthen human rights protections for prospective immigrants, repeal recent changes that encourage rapid deportation, and reverse policies that place barriers on family reunification.
  • They would also restore funding for refugee healthcare and make sure that Canada is doing "everything it can" to take in as many refugees as possible.

Marijuana

The Greens promise to legalize marijuana completely and collect taxes on the industry.

  • Legalize marijuana
  • Believes that drug addictions should be treated as a health problem, not as criminal offences

Environment

Elizabeth May would introduce a carbon fee on fossil fuels. The revenues from this would be returned to all Canadians adults in the form of a carbon dividend. Greens advocate a 40% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions from 2005 levels by 2025 and commit to $500 million each year to the Green Climate fund.

  • Commit Canada at COP21 in Paris this December, to reducing greenhouse gas emissions to 40% below 2005 levels by 2025. To accomplish these reductions, they would establish a revenue-neutral Carbon Fee and Dividend system to phase out carbon emissions and promote renewable energy.
  • Spend $500 million each year to the Green Climate fund
  • Embed the "right to a healthy environment" in Canada’s constitution
  • Cancel the $1.3 billion dollar annual subsidy to fossil fuel companies
  • Committed to strengthening the polluter-pay principle
